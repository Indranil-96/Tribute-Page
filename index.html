Subhas Chandra Bose

Netaji was born on 23rd January 1897 into welth and privilage in a large Bengali family in odisha and died August 18, 1945 in Taiwan.

He was a great Indian nationalist and fought numerous battle agaist britist and also known as Prince among the Patriots.

Bose studied at the prestigious Presidency College, Calcutta, from which he was later expelled in 1916 on the grounds of nationalist activities.

Bose attended the University of Cambridge, England to prepare for the Indian Civil Service.

In 1919, he ranked 4th in the esteemed Indian Civil Services exam.

He was elected as the President of the All India National Congress twice.

Subhash Chandra Bose was married to an Austrian woman named Emilie Schenkl.

In 1924, Bose was appointed as the chief executive officer of the Calcutta Municipal Corporation.

Subhas Chandra Bose gave the famous slogun "Tum Mujhe Khun DO, Main Tumhe Azadi Dunga".

He started the popular newspaper "Swaraj".

Bose worked as a journalist and commandant of the Bengal Congress Volunteers.

During exile, Bose wrote The Indian Struggle, an analysis of the Indian Independence Movement.
